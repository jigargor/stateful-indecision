# Science of Science Notes

The science of science studies how research systems allocate attention, credibility, and effort. A recurring theme is path dependence: early framing choices can shape what later becomes measurable. Systems that preserve intermediate hypotheses often recover from framing errors more effectively.

In this synthetic document, a small model ecosystem used periodic reflective checkpoints and shallow policy baselines. The baseline reduced confounding during debugging: unusual outcomes were easier to attribute to prompt behavior, environmental constraints, or logging gaps rather than hidden policy drift.
